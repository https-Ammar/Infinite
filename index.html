Frequently Asked Questions

Click on a question to view the answer

What if I have dietary restrictions or follow a special diet? +
The test helps you personalize your diet safely. Even if you follow keto, vegan, or gluten-free plans, your results will reveal which foods still trigger inflammation or immune reactions.
Will insurance cover the test? +
Many insurance plans with out-of-network benefits reimburse part of the cost. For those without coverage, we provide transparent cash-pay options with fast turnaround.
How is this different from a typical food intolerance or allergy test? +
Most standard tests only check IgE or lactose/gluten intolerance. The F.A.S.T. test analyzes four immune pathways (IgE, IgG, IgG4, C3b/d), revealing both immediate and delayed reactions that other tests often miss.
Can I collect my sample at home? +
Yes. We offer a dried blood spot (DBS) collection option that's easy to use and safe to mail back to our lab. Instructions are included in every kit.
How do I know if my symptoms are food-related or hormonal? +
The F.A.S.T. test helps uncover inflammation patterns linked to foods or environmental triggers. If your report shows low or no food reactions, your provider may recommend hormone or metabolic testing for a clearer picture.
How do I prepare for the test? +
No fasting or food restrictions are required. Continue your usual diet and lifestyle as this ensures accurate detection of real immune responses.
What kind of support will I receive after the test? +
After receiving your report, you'll have the option to schedule a detailed review session with one of our registered dietitians. They'll help you understand your results and design a personalized plan for better digestion, energy, and overall health.
What if I have a question or need more information? +
We'd love to help you understand your results or guide you through the next step of your wellness journey. Simply fill out the quick form below, and one of our specialists will personally reach out to answer your questions and support you in getting started.
